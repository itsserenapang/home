Veterans Affairs Canada

For the past 2 years, I've worked full time from June to August under the Canada Summer Jobs Program for the Royal Canadian Legion Branch 375.
In a diverse team of 5, we completed historical work like digitalizing veteran records from WWI and WW2, creating recognition banners, archive analysis, and many more. Under the supervision of Legion Treasurer, Ross Toms, I picked up vital technical skills like working with basic machinery, using various Adobe applications, and further developed my leadership and team-building abilities.

2019-2020

For my most recent term in the Canada Summer Jobs Program, I created a 100+ slide Powerpoint that details the biography of every person on the Richmond Hill Cenotaph. In addition to this, I updated the Canadian Virtual War Memorial with veterans we've found and have archived data for.
As the Communications Systems Support, I was often tasked with managing technical issues among our team. In addition to updating and maintaining the organization website on WordPress, I solved issues with printers, computers, and applications with our older staff members.

2018-2019

During my first term in the Canada Summer Jobs Program, I digitalized the accounting process for the Royal Canadian Legion. This included data entry and MS Excel manipulation for approximately 12 months of purchases/sales/etc and a yearly inventory count.
In addition to the accounting work, our team visited cemeteries to find previously unrecognized veterans. Using a variety of data research techniques and archive retrieval from various historians, we found over 200 previously unknown veterans spanning the War of 1812 to present day.
